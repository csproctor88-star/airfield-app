# DTO Meeting — Talking Points & Demo Flow
## 30-Minute Virtual Presentation

---

## PRE-MEETING PREP

- Have Glidepath open on Selfridge installation (your primary demo base)
- Have a second tab ready on Volk Field to show multi-base capability
- Close unnecessary browser tabs
- Share your screen showing the Airfield Status page when the meeting starts
- Have the Executive Summary PDF ready to drop in the Teams chat at the end

## OPENING (0-3 minutes)

**Set the stage — you are the subject matter expert, not a vendor.**

> "Thank you for taking the time to meet with me. I'm MSgt Proctor — I'm an Airfield Manager at Selfridge Air National Guard Base in Michigan. I've been in airfield management for [X] years, and about [X] months ago I started building a digital platform to solve problems I was dealing with every single day on the airfield.

> The core problem is this: Airfield Managers are responsible for every aspect of airfield operations — runway status, inspections, discrepancy tracking, NOTAMs, emergency checklists, obstruction evaluations, wildlife management, waivers — and we do all of it with paper logs, Excel spreadsheets, and disconnected systems. There's no unified digital tool built for what we do. Every base reinvents the wheel.

> What I built is called Glidepath. It's a web-based platform that consolidates all of those functions into one application. It's in operational use at two bases today, and I'm here to get your advice on how to take it enterprise."

## LIVE DEMO — OPERATIONAL HUB (3-8 minutes)

**Show the Airfield Status page. Let them absorb it.**

> "This is the Airfield Status page — it's the first thing an Airfield Manager sees when they open the app. Everything they need is right here."

Point out:
- **Live weather** — auto-updating from Open-Meteo
- **Runway status** — Open/Suspended/Closed with one click, auto-logged
- **NAVAID panels** — green/yellow/red toggles for every NAVAID
- **ARFF readiness** — vehicle status at a glance
- **Personnel on Airfield** — who's out there right now
- **KPI badges** — inspection status, open discrepancies, shift checklist progress

> "Every status change pushes to all connected users in real time. If I change the runway to Suspended, every Airfield Manager logged in sees it instantly."

---

## LIVE DEMO — DAILY OPERATIONS (8-13 minutes)

**Quick flow — don't deep-dive, show the connected ecosystem.**

1. **Events Log** — "This replaces the paper AF Form 3616. Every action is auto-logged with Zulu timestamps, operating initials, and color-coded by type. Templates for common entries like shift changes, NOTAM actions, SCN checks."

2. **Discrepancies** — Show the list view with KPI badges, then flip to Map View. "This is the common operating picture — every open discrepancy plotted on satellite imagery with severity colors. CES has their own dashboard filtered to their shop."

3. **Checks** — "Seven check types in one form — FOD, RSC, BASH, IFE, Ground Emergency, Heavy Aircraft. Photos, GPS pins, auto-generated discrepancies."

> "The key insight here is that everything is connected. A failed inspection item creates a discrepancy. A discrepancy links to a CES work order. An outage on a NAVAID creates a discrepancy and triggers a DAFMAN compliance alert. Nothing falls through the cracks."

## LIVE DEMO — DECISION SUPPORT (13-17 minutes)

**These are the differentiators — no one else has these.**

1. **Obstruction Evaluation Tool** — Click a point on the satellite map. "Instant UFC 3-260-01 imaginary surface analysis against all runways simultaneously. Distance from centerline, distance from threshold, surface zone, violation detection with UFC table references. This analysis used to take 30 minutes with a calculator — now it's one click."

2. **Visual NAVAIDs** — Show the infrastructure map with lights plotted. "Every airfield light digitized on satellite imagery. The DAFMAN 13-204v2 outage engine tracks which systems are degraded and prescribes the required actions. When a light is marked inoperative, a discrepancy is auto-created."

3. **Analytics Dashboard** — "30-day metrics across every operational area. Leadership can see trends without asking for a report."

## THE ASK (20-25 minutes)

**Be direct about what you need.**

> "I built this to solve a real problem, and it's working. Two bases are using it operationally, and I'm getting interest from others across the career field. I want to make this available to every airfield management section in the DAF — for free. I'm not trying to sell anything. I just want to get it into the hands of the people who need it.

> The challenge is that I'm an Airfield Manager, not an acquisitions officer. I don't know the right process to go from 'it works at two bases' to 'it's available enterprise-wide.' That's why I'm here — I need your advice.

> **First** — I've been looking at Platform One's Party Bus program as a path to a cATO. Is that the right approach? And if so, who should I be talking to?

> **Second** — What does the process look like for making a free, independently-developed tool available across the DAF? Is there a precedent for this?

> **Third** — Who are the right stakeholders to engage? I think AF/A3, AFCEC, or AFIMSC might own this mission area, but I'm not sure who would champion something like this."

---

## DISCUSSION (25-30 minutes)

**Let them talk. Take notes. Ask:**

- "What pathway do you recommend for something like this?"
- "Are there other grassroots tools that have successfully gone enterprise? What did that look like?"
- "Who else should I be talking to?"
- "Is there anything about the technical approach that would need to change?"
- "What's the biggest hurdle you've seen for projects like this?"

---

## CLOSING

> "I'll drop the executive summary in the chat. I appreciate your time and any guidance you can provide. This career field needs a digital transformation, and I believe Glidepath is the answer."

---

## DO's AND DON'Ts

**Do:**
- Speak as an operator, not a developer
- Use operational language ("the airfield manager sees...", "during a shift change...")
- Show real data from Selfridge (sanitize if needed)
- Be honest about where you are (2 bases, built by one person, no ATO yet)
- Ask for advice, not approval

**Don't:**
- Don't try to show every feature — pick the highlights
- Don't get into technical architecture (unless they ask)
- Don't apologize for it being a grassroots effort — that's a strength
- Don't oversell — "operational at 2 bases with interest from more" is honest and compelling
- Don't spend time on the obstruction polygon alignment issue
